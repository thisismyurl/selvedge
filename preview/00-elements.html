Body

Natural dyeing is the practice of using plant-based pigments to colour textile fibers. Unlike synthetic dyes, plant-based colours develop a patina over time — deepening, shifting, becoming more themselves as the fabric is worn and washed. This is the quality that makes naturally dyed textiles worth the labour.

The mordanting process binds the dye to the fiber. Common mordants include alum, iron, and tannin-rich oak gall. Each mordant shifts the final colour differently.

Dyestuffs

  • Japanese Indigo (Persicaria tinctoria)
  • Weld (Reseda luteola)
  • Woad (Isatis tinctoria)
  • Madder root
  • Black-eyed Susan

Process

  1. Scour the fiber
  2. Apply mordant
  3. Prepare the dye bath
  4. Enter and exhaust the bath
