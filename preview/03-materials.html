Materials & Dyes

The dye floor.

Every colour here is grown, foraged, or fermented. The swatch is the truth; the recipe is the process. Each material is woven to a finished bolt before it leaves the studio.

Indigo
Fermented indigo linen
Vat dye · 6 dips

A week-long ferment yields a blue that oxidises in air as the cloth lifts from the vat. Each dip deepens the warp.

Weld
Weld-dyed wool
Plant dye · Alum mordant

Weld gives the oldest clear yellow in the European dye tradition. Mordanted wool holds it through a hundred washes.

Madder
Madder-root rose
Root dye · Slow simmer

Madder root, never boiled, draws a warm rose from the fibre. Heat it too hard and the red turns brick.

Walnut
Walnut-hull brown
Bark dye · No mordant

Walnut hull is a substantive dye — it needs no mordant to fix. The brown shifts cooler as the bath ages.

Undyed
Loom-state hemp
Natural fibre · Unbleached

Hemp warp left exactly as the field gave it. The greenish cast softens to oat after the first wash.

Overdye
Indigo-over-weld green
Double dye · Blue over yellow

No single plant gives a true green; you build it. Weld first, indigo over — the order is the recipe.
